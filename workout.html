Mark's 5-Day Plan

Hypertrophy · Full Commercial Gym · Revised

1

Push

Chest · Shoulders · Triceps
Session note: Lateral raises are the priority movement here — treat them with the same focus as the compound lifts. Aim for 50–65 min total.
1 Incline Dumbbell Press
4 sets6–10▼

90–120 sec

3 sec down / 1 sec up

RIR 2 on sets 1–3, RIR 1 on set 4

Rep range dropped to 6–10 for better strength-hypertrophy overlap

  • Slight arch, chest up, scapulae retracted
  • Lower to nipple line, not collarbone
  • Drive elbows slightly inward on the way up
2 Cable Chest Fly
3 sets12–15▼

60 sec

RIR 1–2

Isolation after compound while chest is freshest

Set cables above shoulder height for lower chest bias

  • Slight bend in elbows — locked in, not moving
  • Emphasise the stretch at the start position
  • Squeeze and pause 1 sec at centre
3 Dumbbell Lateral Raise Priority
4 sets12–20▼

45–60 sec

RIR 1 — should genuinely burn

Lighter than you think. Most people go 20–30% too heavy.

Try cable lateral raise on last set for constant tension

  • Lean very slightly forward — keeps tension on the lateral head
  • Lead with your elbows, not your hands
  • Slow on the way down — 3 sec lowering phase
  • Stop at shoulder height, no higher
4 Seated DB Overhead Press
3 sets8–10▼

90 sec

After laterals your side delts are warm — press with more mind-muscle connection

RIR 2 — no failure

  • Don't lock out at the top — keep tension on the shoulder
  • Neutral spine, don't hyperextend lower back
5 Overhead DB Triceps Extension
3 sets10–12▼

60 sec

Long head gets a full stretch overhead — best mass builder for triceps

  • Feel the deep stretch at the bottom — don't rush through it
  • Elbows pointing forward, not flaring out
6 Cable Triceps Pushdown
3 sets12–15▼

45–60 sec

Finish the triceps — RIR 1

  • Elbows pinned to sides
  • Full extension, squeeze at bottom
  • Use rope for better contraction
2

Pull

Back · Biceps · Rear Delts
Session note: Back width and thickness both need hitting. Lead with vertical pull, then horizontal. Biceps are last — they'll be pre-fatigued from rows. That's fine.
1 Weighted Pull-Up / Lat Pulldown
4 sets6–10▼

90–120 sec

If pull-ups: add weight when you hit 10 clean reps

RIR 2 sets 1–3, RIR 1 on set 4

  • Initiate by depressing the scapulae before pulling with arms
  • Pull to upper chest, not chin
  • Control the negative — 3 sec down
2 Chest-Supported Row
4 sets8–12▼

90 sec

Back thickness needs more volume — this is your main horizontal pull

Neutral or pronated — try both and pick what you feel more in your back

  • Chest pinned to pad throughout — no momentum
  • Squeeze shoulder blades together at the top
  • Think about pulling your elbows to your hips, not your hands to your chest
3 Face Pull
3 sets15–20▼

45 sec

Rear delt + rotator cuff health. Don't skip this.

  • Cable at forehead height
  • Pull to your ears — elbows high and wide
  • External rotate at the end so your thumbs point behind you
4 Rear Delt DB Fly
3 sets12–15▼

45 sec

Very light. Most people use 3–5x too much.

  • Bend forward to at least 45°, ideally chest to thighs
  • Pinky higher than thumb at the top
  • No swinging — if you are, drop the weight
5 Incline DB Curl
3 sets10–12▼

60 sec

Long head stretch position — best bicep mass exercise

  • Arms hang straight down off bench — full stretch
  • Supinate wrist as you curl
  • No swinging, no shoulder involvement
6 Hammer Curl
3 sets10–12▼

45 sec

Brachialis — thickens the arm under the bicep peak

  • Neutral grip throughout — no rotation
  • Alternate arms or do together, your preference
3

Legs + Core

Quads · Hamstrings · Calves · Core
Session note: This is the hardest day. Don't skip it — legs are the biggest muscle groups and the biggest driver of overall growth and hormonal response. Hack squat added as primary if available.
1 Hack Squat / Leg Press
4 sets8–12▼

2–3 min

Hack squat if available — greater quad stimulus than leg press

RIR 2 on sets 1–3, RIR 1 on set 4

Shoulder-width, slight toe-out

  • Full depth — thighs at least parallel
  • Don't lock knees hard at the top
  • Drive through your full foot, not just toes
2 Romanian Deadlift
3 sets8–10▼

2 min

RIR 2 — never to failure on this movement

The hamstring stretch, not the weight

  • Hip hinge — push hips back, not down
  • Bar stays close to legs throughout
  • Feel the stretch at the bottom — pause briefly
  • Soft knees, not locked
3 Leg Curl (Seated or Lying)
3 sets10–12▼

60–75 sec

Hamstrings need direct work in both stretch and contraction positions

  • Squeeze hard at peak contraction
  • 3 sec lowering phase
  • Don't let hips rise on the seated version
4 Walking Lunges
3 sets10–12 / leg▼

90 sec

DBs held at sides or barbell on back

  • Front knee tracks over second toe
  • Torso upright — don't lean forward
  • Control the descent
5 Standing Calf Raise
4 sets12–15▼

45–60 sec

Calves are absent from original plan — a notable gap

  • Full range — deep stretch at bottom, full contraction at top
  • Hold at the top for 1 sec
  • Slow and controlled beats heavy and bouncy every time
6 Cable Crunch + Plank
3 sets12–15 / 45–60s▼

45 sec between pairs

Superset these — crunch then immediately plank

  • Cable crunch: round the spine, don't hip hinge
  • Plank: glutes and abs both braced

Global Rules

Apply Every Session

Key Changes from Original

  • Calves added to Day 3 — they were missing entirely
  • Day 1 order revised: fly moved before shoulder press for better chest isolation while fresh
  • Overhead triceps extension moved before pushdown — long head gets priority
  • Chest-supported row bumped to 4 sets — back thickness needs the volume
  • Day 4 skull crushers replace rope pushdown — better long head stimulus
  • Day 5 refocused as shoulders + arms specialisation — not a random mix
  • Rep ranges on primary lifts standardised to 6–10 for better strength-hypertrophy overlap

Effort (RIR)

  • Sets 1–3: stop 2 reps before failure
  • Final set: stop 1 rep before failure
  • Never grind to failure on compounds
  • Isolation last sets can go to failure occasionally

Tempo

  • Concentric (lift): 1–2 sec
  • Eccentric (lower): 2–3 sec
  • Isolation exercises: 3 sec eccentric
  • Never drop the weight — the negative matters

Progression

  • Hit top of rep range → add weight next session
  • Track your weights — even just on your phone
  • Don't increase weight if form breaks down
  • Deload every 6–8 weeks (50% volume)

Session Length

  • Days 1–2: 55–70 min realistic
  • Day 3: 60–75 min (legs take longer)
  • Day 4: 55–65 min
  • Day 5: 40–50 min
